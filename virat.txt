Virat Kohli is a celebrated Indian international cricketer, widely regarded as one of the greatest batsmen in history. He is known for his aggressive batting style, incredible fitness, and leadership as a former captain across all three formats of international cricket. Kohli's legacy extends beyond his many records, as he is credited with transforming the work ethic and fitness standards of Indian cricket. 
Career and achievements
Early success: Kohli gained fame as the captain who led India to victory in the 2008 Under-19 World Cup. He made his senior debut in the same year and was part of the 2011 World Cup-winning team.
Captaincy: He captained the Indian national team in Test, ODI, and T20I formats, becoming the nation's most successful Test captain with 40 victories.
Batting records: He holds numerous batting records, including being the fastest to score many ODI centuries and a significant number of runs. He is also the leading run-scorer in the history of the IPL.
International awards: He was named the ICC "Cricketer of the Year" in 2017 and 2018 and was recognized as the "Leading Cricketer in the World" by Wisden Cricketers' Almanack in 2017, 2018, and 2019.
